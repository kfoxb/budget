# Utah Home Garden ROI Analysis: Maximizing Grocery Bill Offset
## Orem, Utah | USDA Zone 6b/7a | ~4,500ft Elevation
### Lean FIRE / Frugality Optimization

---

## Methodology

Values below are compiled from USDA ERS data, BLS average retail prices (2025-2026), Amazon/retail freeze-dried pricing, Bonnie Plants efficiency ratings, university extension yield data, and square foot gardening yield reports. All prices reflect US average retail; Utah prices track close to national average (slightly below in summer due to local agriculture).

**Key assumptions:**
- Intensive raised bed / square foot gardening methods
- Good soil with compost amendments (critical in Utah's alkaline clay)
- Drip irrigation (essential in Utah's arid climate)
- Vertical growing where applicable (trellises for tomatoes, cucumbers, beans)
- Growing season: ~May 1 - Oct 15 (170 days), with season extension possible

## Part 2: Freeze-Dry Value Analysis

**This is where your Harvest Right becomes a money printer.**

The weight ratio for freeze drying is roughly 7-10:1 (7-10 lbs fresh = 1 lb freeze-dried) for most fruits, and 5-8:1 for vegetables.

### Priority List for Your Freeze Dryer (garden crops)

1. **Peaches** - You already have the tree. FD peaches retail at $35-56/lb. A productive peach tree yields 100-200 lbs/year. Even at 10:1 ratio, that's 10-20 lbs of FD peaches worth $350-1,120.
2. **Strawberries** - FD strawberries at $27-68/lb retail. Grow everbearing varieties.
3. **Raspberries** - If you plant canes, FD raspberries at $45-88/lb retail is the highest per-pound value.
4. **Hot peppers** - Lightweight, high FD value, prolific in Utah.
5. **Herbs** - FD herbs retain flavor better than air-dried. Extremely high value per weight.
6. **Cherry tomatoes** - FD cherry tomato halves are a premium snack product.
7. **Snap peas** - Excellent FD snack, high retail value.
8. **Bell peppers** - FD diced peppers are a cooking staple worth $28-40/lb.

## Part 6: Key Strategic Insights for Lean FIRE

### The Big Wins

1. **Your freeze dryer is the #1 asset.** The markup on freeze-dried fruit is 8-20x fresh price. Every pound of strawberries, raspberries, or peaches you grow and freeze-dry is worth $30-80 in retail equivalent. Even buying fresh fruit on sale and freeze-drying it is profitable vs. buying FD retail.

2. **Your peach tree is an underappreciated goldmine.** A productive peach tree yielding 150 lbs = 15 lbs freeze-dried peaches = $525-840 retail equivalent. Plus canned peach butter, peach jam, peach salsa. This single tree could offset $500-1,000/year.

3. **Herbs are dollar-for-dollar the best garden ROI.** A $0.15 basil seed becomes a plant that produces $30-60 of grocery store basil. Even a small herb area dramatically reduces grocery spending.

4. **Tomatoes + canning = pantry independence.** 20 tomato plants can produce 200+ lbs. Canned into sauce, salsa, diced tomatoes, and paste, this replaces $200-400 of canned goods annually.

5. **Plant raspberries and blackberries NOW.** Perennial, low-maintenance after establishment, and the freeze-dried value is the highest of any garden crop per pound. A 20-foot raspberry row producing 20 lbs/year = 2 lbs FD = $90-176 retail equivalent, every year, forever.

### Crops to SKIP (low ROI per square foot)

- **Corn**: 2 ears per sq ft, $0.50 value. Terrible ROI unless you have unlimited space.
- **Potatoes**: $1.20/lb retail, 1.5 lb/sq ft = $1.80/sq ft. Cheap to buy.
- **Winter squash / Pumpkins**: Sprawling, low value per area. Unless trellised.
- **Melons**: Huge space hogs. Buy at farmers market.
- **Cabbage / Cauliflower**: Long growing season, moderate yield, cheap to buy.

### Utah-Specific Tips

- **Alkaline soil**: Amend heavily with compost and sulfur. Blueberries are nearly impossible without extreme soil modification; skip them and buy fresh to FD instead.
- **Water**: Drip irrigation is non-negotiable. Mulch heavily to conserve moisture. Budget $15-25/month for water during summer.
- **Heat**: Tomatoes, peppers, squash, and beans LOVE Utah summers. Take advantage of the 90-100F days.
- **Short season**: Start tomatoes and peppers indoors in March. Use row cover/cold frames for early spring greens and late fall crops.
- **Fall planting garlic**: Plant garlic cloves in October for a June harvest. Zero effort over winter.

### Preservation Strategy Ranking (by grocery bill offset)

1. **Freeze dry all berries and peaches** (highest markup per lb)
2. **Can tomato sauce, salsa, and diced tomatoes** (replaces expensive canned goods year-round)
3. **Freeze dry herbs** (retains flavor, extremely high value per weight)
4. **Pickle cucumbers and peppers** (water bath canning, easy, high value)
5. **Pressure can green beans** (pantry staple replacement)
6. **Freeze dry bell peppers and hot peppers** (lightweight, high retail value)
7. **Freeze dry snap peas, corn** (premium snack items)
8. **Root cellar garlic, carrots, beets** (natural storage, no processing needed)
