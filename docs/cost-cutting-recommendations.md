# Cost-Cutting Recommendations (Mustachian Analysis)

## Priority 1: High Impact, Easy Wins

### 1. Kill DoorDash (~$3,200/yr saved)
- Average order: $52 including fees/tips
- Same meal cooked at home: $8-15
- Jessica already preserves food and gardens -- the kitchen skills are there
- Freeze dryer can batch-prep meals for busy nights
- **Action**: Delete the app. Batch cook on weekends. Use freeze dryer for emergency "lazy night" meals.

### 2. Cut Eating Out by 60% (~$6,000/yr saved)
- Current: $828/mo across 68 different restaurants in 6 months
- Target: $300/mo (date nights + occasional family treat)
- With canning, freeze drying, and gardening, Jessica has a food production system most families would kill for
- **Action**: Set a firm $300/mo eating out budget in YNAB. Plan meals weekly.

### 3. Cancel Starlink (~$192/yr saved)
- Starlink is for mobile/occasional use but now charges a monthly retainer even when not in use
- XMission ($59/mo) is the primary home ISP -- keep that
- If mobile internet is needed occasionally, phone hotspot is free with most plans
- **Action**: Cancel Starlink, use phone hotspot for the rare mobile need

### 4. Pick One AI Tool (~$250/yr saved)
- Paying for both Claude.ai ($40/mo) and ChatGPT ($21/mo)
- As a software engineer, pick the one that works best for your workflow
- Claude Code (CLI) comes with Pro/Max subscription -- likely more useful for engineering

### 5. Cancel Car Wash Subscription (~$550/yr saved)
- $46/mo for Quick Quack
- Wash at home with a bucket, hose, and $5 of supplies
- Kids can "help" -- it becomes family activity instead of expense

### 6. Rotate Streaming Services (~$300/yr saved)
- Currently: Spotify ($23) + Google TV ($20) + Starz ($12) = $55/mo
- Keep Spotify (family plan is worth it with kids)
- Rotate Google TV and Starz -- subscribe to one for a month, binge, cancel, switch
- Check if Orem Public Library offers free streaming (Kanopy, Libby, Hoopla)

## Priority 2: Medium Impact, More Effort

### 7. Optimize Grocery Strategy
- Current: $1,186/mo across Walmart, Costco, Smith's, WinCo, Sprouts
- WinCo is the cheapest -- shift more shopping there
- Costco: great for bulk staples, but easy to overspend on impulse
- Use the garden and preservation skills to offset produce costs
- **Freeze dryer opportunity**: Buy bulk produce in season, freeze dry for year-round use
- Target: $900/mo (-$286/mo = $3,432/yr saved)

### 8. PetSmart Optimization (~$600-1,000/yr saved)
- $195/mo is high for dog expenses
- Switch to Chewy.com autoship (typically 5-30% cheaper than PetSmart)
- Buy food at Costco (Kirkland brand dog food is well-rated and much cheaper)
- Buy treats/chews in bulk online

### 9. Leverage the Freeze Dryer for Meal Prep
- Freeze-dried meals cost $8-15 retail per serving
- Home freeze-dried meals cost ~$2-3 per serving
- Batch cook soups, stews, chili, pasta dishes on weekends
- Vacuum seal and store -- instant "fast food" that just needs hot water
- This directly replaces DoorDash and reduces eating out temptation

## Priority 3: Tech/Software Solutions (Kyle's Raspberry Pis)

### 10. Self-Host Services on Raspberry Pi
- **Pi-hole**: Block ads network-wide (saves on any ad-supported tier upgrades)
- **Jellyfin/Plex**: Self-hosted media server -- rip DVDs, manage digital library, reduce streaming dependency
- **Mealie/Tandoor**: Self-hosted recipe manager -- meal planning reduces food waste and impulse ordering
- **Grocy**: Self-hosted grocery/pantry management -- track what's in the pantry, freezer, and freeze dryer inventory to avoid duplicate purchases
- **Home Assistant**: Automate thermostat, lights, etc. for utility savings
- **Uptime Kuma**: Monitor your self-hosted services
- **Vaultwarden**: Self-hosted password manager (replace any paid password manager)

### 11. Automate Budget Alerts
- You already have YNAB API access (this repo!)
- Build a script that sends alerts when:
  - Eating out budget hits 50% before mid-month
  - DoorDash transaction detected (shame alert)
  - Any single transaction > $200 (cooling-off notification)
- Run on a Raspberry Pi as a cron job
